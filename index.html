Timeline of Zora Neale Hurston's Professional Life

  • 1891 - Zora Neale Hurston born (as Zora Lee Hurston) on January 7 in Notasulga, Alabama, fifth child of Lucy Ann Potts and John Hurston.
  • 1894 - Hurston family moves to Eatonville, Florida, a small all-black community five miles north of Orlando, Florida.
  • 1897 - Hurston's father is elected mayor of Eatonville.
  • 1904 - Hurston's mother dies on September 18; Hurston is sent in October to school in Jacksonville.
  • 1906-1915 - Hurston lives with various members of her family, works as a domestic.
  • 1915-1916 - Hurston travels with a Gilbert & Sullivan troupe as maid to lead singer.
  • 1917 - Hurston enters Morgan Academy (high school division of what is now Morgan State University).
  • 1918 - Hurston graduates in June from Morgan Academy.
  • 1920 - Hurston receives an associate degree from Howard University where she has majored in English and studied with black linguist Lorenzo Dow Turner.
  • 1921 - Hurston's first story, "John Redding Goes to Sea" published in Stylus, Howard University's literary club's magazine.
  • 1924 - Hurston publishes short story "Drenched in Light" in Opportunity, literary journal of the Urban League.
  • 1925 - Hurston moves to New York City; in September enters Barnard College as its only African American student and studies anthropology with Franz Boas.
  • 1934 - Hurston publishes her first novel, Jonah's Gourd Vine.
  • 1937 - Their Eyes Were Watching God, Hurston's most famous novel, is published in September.
  • 1948 - Hurston's third novel, Seraph on the Suwanee is published in October.
